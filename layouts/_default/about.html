About

I like work that sits between technical questions and real-world decisions. I’ve worked with causal inference, machine learning, econometrics, and open source software, but I’ve also worked closely with banks, institutional investors, construction crews, and supported real large capital decisions. That overlap is where I do my best work.

Early Design

In high school, I took part in a space settlement competition organized by NASA. I had to figure out where people would live, how things would be built, and how everything would work day to day.

UBC

At UBC, I studied finance, math, philosophy, history, and political science. I spent a lot of time doing research, making friends, and taking long walks around campus.

Chard

After graduation, I helped build homes and communities at Chard. I joined at a busy time, so I ended up doing a bit of everything: visiting construction sites, working with banks and investors, doing market research, and thinking through the decisions behind large buildings.
