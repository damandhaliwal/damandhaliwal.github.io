In high school, I took part in a space settlement competition organized by NASA. I had to figure out where people would live, how things would be built, and how everything would work day to day.

At UBC, I studied finance, math, philosophy, history, and political science. I spent a lot of time doing research, making friends, and taking long walks around campus.

After graduation, I helped build homes and communities at Chard.

I joined at a busy time, so I ended up doing a bit of everything: visiting construction sites, working with banks and investors, doing market research, and thinking through the decisions behind large buildings.

These days, I’m wrapping up my master’s in economics at the University of Toronto. I’ve been writing research papers, working with data and models, and building tools and workflows that make the work easier.
